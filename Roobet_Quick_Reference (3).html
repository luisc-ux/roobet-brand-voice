On Edge & Innuendo
Roobet has permission to live close to a line. Copy can carry a double reading, as long as it's smart enough that the joke works either way.

The test: remove the second reading. Does the line still work? If yes, you're good. If it only works as innuendo, it's too much.

✓ Works in: CRM body, onsite inbox, sports campaigns.
✗ Not in: welcome emails, ACQ, or subject lines without context.
Universal Rules
One ! per piece. If it doesn't earn it, cut it.
No em dashes. Ever. Use a period, comma, or restructure.
Never "chase" in CTAs.
"Don't Like Fun?": unsubscribe line. Never change it.
Welcome emails: "Crypto's Fastest Growing Casino" + specific promise immediately after.
Words & Phrases to Avoid
stake
Avoid entirely: verb and competing brand reference.
log in (to logged-in users)
They're already in. Confusing at best, lazy at worst.
Don't miss out!
Generic casino panic. Be specific about what they're missing.
Huge rewards await
Vague. Replace with the actual number.
This is your chance!
Every casino says this. The copy itself should create urgency.
Amazing / incredible
Replace with a specific fact. Facts beat adjectives.
We miss you!
Try: "Your account's been quiet. We put something in it."
WIN BIG / FREE!!! / GUARANTEED
Spam triggers. Kills deliverability instantly.
LIMITED TIME OFFER
"Closes Sunday" beats "limited time" every time.
Exclusive offer
Prove it: say who it's for and why.
Click here
"Claim your $20" beats "click here" every time.
contendientes (ES)
Doesn't land naturally in LATAM markets.

Email Deliverability

Deliverability Rules: Protect Inbox Placement
Subject Line
No $ amounts or currency figures. No % figures. One emoji max. Filters detect the presence of an emoji, not which one. The limit is on quantity, not type. Lead with narrative or curiosity, not financial value. Personalization tokens are fine.
Pre-header
Read alongside the SL by Gmail's algorithm. Don't use the PH to sneak in prize amounts cut from the SL. That workaround doesn't work. Story or intrigue only.
Body Copy
Dollar amounts are fine in the body for existing subscribers. Don't stack prize figures, opt-in language, and "Promotions Page" in the opening lines. Lead with narrative first.
Structure
Always include a plain text alternative. Maintain healthy image-to-text ratio. One large image with minimal text reads as spam.
Words to Avoid in Subject Line & Pre-header
free / bonus / prize
Primary spam filter triggers. Use "reward", "drop", or describe the specific thing instead.
win / jackpot / guaranteed
High-risk trigger words regardless of context. Rephrase around the game or moment instead.
$[any amount] in SL or PH
Financial figures in the subject line are the single biggest promo-tab signal. Move amounts to the body.
cash / exclusive offer / act now
Generic commercial language. Be specific about what, when, and who.
limited time / click here
Both are spam flags. Use specific dates and specific CTAs instead.
Pre-send check: Run GlockApps or mail-tester.com before every send. Monitor domain reputation via Google Postmaster Tools. Safe SL/PH words: tournament, round, stage, points, tonight, your username token, sport/team references, specific dates.

If it could have been written by a competitor, it's not good enough.
